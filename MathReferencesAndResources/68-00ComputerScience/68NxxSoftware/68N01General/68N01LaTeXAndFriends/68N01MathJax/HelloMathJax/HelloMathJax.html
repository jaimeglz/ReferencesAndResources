When $a \ne 0$, there are two solutions to \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\) and they are: $$x = {-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac} \over 2a}.$$

These are integrals:

$$ \int \sqrt{\frac{1}{x}}\, dx $$ $$ \int_{0}^{\pi} \cos^{2}{\left (x \right )}\, dx $$ Logic: $$ y \vee \left(x \wedge y\right) $$
